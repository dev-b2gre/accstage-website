VILLA NO. 7

A brutalist sanctuary carved from raw concrete and light.

Location Kyoto, Japan
Year 2023
Area 8,500 SQFT
Category Residential

The Concept of Weight

Challenging the fragility of modern domestic structures through the uncompromising permanence of monolithic form.

Villa No. 7 was conceived as an anchor against the ephemeral nature of contemporary life. By utilizing slip-formed concrete as the primary structural and aesthetic material, the residence demands a physical engagement from its inhabitants. The walls are not merely partitions; they are geological formations within the landscape.

"We wanted to build something that felt inevitable. A structure that didn't just sit on the earth, but felt like it had been excavated from it."

— Elias Thorne, Lead Architect

The interplay of light across the vast, unadorned surfaces creates a shifting gallery of shadows throughout the day. Deep overhangs protect the interior from harsh midday sun while drawing the gaze outward toward the meticulously curated Zen gardens, blurring the boundary between the austere interior and the organic exterior.

Material Study

Formwork lines were intentionally retained, serving as a permanent record of the construction process and imparting a tactile grain to the space.

Spatial Rhythm

Transitional spaces are compressed and expanded intentionally. The central corridor acts as a decompression zone, a sheer volume of negative space that cleanses the visual palette before entering the primary living areas.
