Set against the harsh, unforgiving topography of the Rocky Mountains, The Oak Residence was conceived not as an intrusion upon the landscape, but as a geological extension of it. The monolithic form prioritizes shelter and permanence.

Our primary challenge was executing sheer structural scale without compromising the intimate, tactile experience of the interior. We utilized a radical palette of board-formed concrete, darkened steel, and native oak, creating a spatial narrative that feels simultaneously imposing and protective.

LEAD ARCHITECT: MARCUS VANCE

MATERIAL COMPOSITION

EXTERIOR FAÇADE

Custom board-formed concrete mix engineered for sub-zero thermal mass. Oxidized Corten steel panelling detailing.

GLAZING

Triple-paned, thermally broken structural glass. Custom blackened steel mullion extrusions.

INTERIOR FINISHES

Reclaimed European Oak wide-plank flooring. Tadelakt plaster walls. Custom patinated brass hardware.
